Module 1 · Weeks 1–3
The Consulting Mindset & Identity Shift
🧠 Foundational ⏱ 3 Weeks 🎯 Beginner
⚡ The Core Shift: A consultant is not paid for what they build — they are paid for the value they create and the decisions they enable. This module rewires how you think about your role.
🔄 The Identity Transformation

After 20+ years as an infrastructure specialist, your instinct is to solve technical problems. As a strategic consultant, your job is to solve business problems using cloud as the instrument. The difference is profound — and it changes everything from how you listen in meetings to how you write proposals.

This module teaches you to think in three layers simultaneously: the technology layer (what AWS services do), the architecture layer (how they fit together), and the business layer (what value they create for the client).

"Clients don't buy technical expertise. They buy confidence that their problems will be solved and their risks will be managed."

— Foundational principle of technology consulting
🏗️
Technical Expert vs. Advisor
Expert: "Here is how VPC Transit Gateway works." Advisor: "Here is why your current network design will block your expansion into 3 new regions and cost you 2.4x more." Learn to lead with impact, not mechanics.
🎯
The Discovery Mindset
Top consultants ask more questions than they answer in initial engagements. Master the art of discovery: understanding the client's pain, fear, ambition, and constraints before proposing any solution.
💼
Scope & Engagement Design
Learn to scope consulting engagements clearly — defining what you will deliver, what success looks like, what is out of scope, and how to protect yourself from scope creep.
🤝
Trusted Advisor Framework
The Trusted Advisor model (Maister et al.): credibility × reliability × intimacy ÷ self-orientation. Understand why the best consultants win long-term relationships, not just projects.

🏗️ Technical + Advisory ⏱ 4 Weeks 🎯 Core Consulting Skill
✅ The WAR is your #1 consulting deliverable. A Well-Architected Review (WAR) is the single most powerful tool you have — it creates a structured, credible, AWS-endorsed assessment of any client's environment and generates a roadmap of improvements (i.e., more consulting work).

📋 How to Conduct a WAR — Step by Step
1
Pre-Engagement: Scope the Workload
Define which workload you are reviewing (a specific application, platform, or business unit). Identify the workload owner, technical lead, and business owner. Book 90-minute workshops — one per pillar. Clarify that this is not an audit; it is a collaborative improvement exercise.
2
Activate the AWS Well-Architected Tool
Log into AWS Console → Well-Architected Tool → Define a Workload. Enter workload name, region, environment type, and team. This tool provides 300+ review questions organized by pillar. The tool generates the report for you automatically.
🔗 Free via: AWS Console → Well-Architected Tool
3
Run Pillar Workshops (90 min each)
For each pillar: review the design principles, walk through the questions with the client team, document current state, identify risk levels (HIGH / MEDIUM / NONE), and capture evidence. Do not let it become a checkbox exercise — dig into the "why" behind each answer.
4
Generate the Risk Report
The AWS tool categorizes findings as High Risk Items (HRIs) and Medium Risk Items (MRIs). Export the report. Typically you will find 15–40 improvement items. Prioritize by impact × effort matrix.
5
Deliver the Executive Findings Presentation
Transform the technical findings into a business narrative. Structure: Current State → Risk Exposure → Priority Improvement Areas → Recommended Roadmap → Investment Summary. This is your consulting deliverable — not the raw report.
📄 Deliverable: WAR Executive Summary (10–15 slides)
📄 WAR Executive Report Structure
Consulting Deliverable Template
Section 1 — Executive Summary (1 page)
Business context, scope of review, top 3 critical findings, recommended next steps.

Section 2 — Current State Assessment
Workload description, risk heat map (6 pillars × risk level), key statistics (X HRIs, Y MRIs found).

Section 3 — Pillar-by-Pillar Findings
For each pillar: Design Principle gaps, specific HRIs with business impact, evidence gathered.

Section 4 — Prioritized Improvement Roadmap
Impact vs. effort matrix, 90-day quick wins, 6-month initiatives, 12-month strategic improvements.

Section 5 — Investment Summary
Estimated cost to remediate (consulting + implementation), estimated risk reduction value.
Practice Scenario
Conduct a Self-WAR on a Demo Workload
AWS provides sample workloads in their Well-Architected Labs. Practice a full review cycle using these free resources before doing it with a real client.
  • Visit: wellarchitectedlabs.com — complete the "Well-Architected Tool" lab (free, ~2 hours)
  • Complete a review on a demo workload using all 6 pillars
  • Write a 2-page mock executive summary for a fictional CTO
  • Time yourself: a real WAR engagement takes 3–5 days of consultant time

🏛️ What is Cloud Governance? (Beginner Explanation)

Governance is the system of rules, accountability, and decision-making structures that ensure cloud resources are used securely, cost-effectively, and in alignment with organizational strategy. Without governance, organizations end up with cloud sprawl, security gaps, and uncontrolled costs.

As a consultant, selling governance advisory is one of the highest-value services you can offer, because it is ongoing, strategic, and non-commoditized.

Module 4 · Weeks 12–15
Executive Communication & Advisory Presence
🎤 Communication ⏱ 4 Weeks 🎯 Career-Defining Skill
💡 The multiplier skill: You can be the most technically brilliant AWS architect in Saudi Arabia — but if you cannot communicate value to a CIO or Minister of Digital Government in their language, you will always be hired as an implementer, never as a strategic advisor. This module changes that.
🔺
The Pyramid Principle
Lead with the answer, then support with argument, then evidence. Never build up to your conclusion — executives have 10 minutes. Example: "My recommendation is Cloud-first. Here are the 3 reasons. Here is the supporting data."
📊
Executive-Level Storytelling
Every briefing needs a narrative arc: Current reality → Risk/Opportunity → Recommended path → What you need from leadership. Data supports the story; it does not replace it.
🎯
The So What? Test
After every statement you plan to make, ask: "So what?" If you cannot answer from the client's perspective, cut that statement. Example: "We migrated 40 servers to AWS." → So what? → "reducing DR recovery time from 8 hours to 15 minutes."
🛡️
Handling Objections
The ACE framework: Acknowledge the concern, Clarify the underlying fear, Explain how your recommendation addresses it. Never argue with a CIO — redirect with curiosity: "That's a critical concern. Can you help me understand what's driving that?"
📝 Executive Briefing Structure — Master This Template
The 5-Slide Executive Briefing
Core Template
Slide 1 — Situation (30 seconds): Where you are today. One paragraph, no jargon.

Slide 2 — Risk / Opportunity (1 minute): What happens if you do nothing vs. what you gain by acting. Use SAR figures, not percentages alone.

Slide 3 — Recommendation (1 minute): Your single clear recommendation. Not 3 options — one recommendation with options only if asked.

Slide 4 — Implementation Path (30 seconds): Phase 1 (quick wins), Phase 2 (core), Phase 3 (optimization). Timeline and owner for each.

Slide 5 — Decision Required: Explicit ask. "We need approval for SAR X to proceed to Phase 1 by [date]." Never leave a meeting without a clear next step.

Module 5 · Weeks 16–19
TCO Analysis & Cloud Business Cases
💰 Financial Advisory ⏱ 4 Weeks 🎯 Deal-Winning Skill
💰 What is TCO Analysis? (Beginner Start)

Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) is a financial comparison between keeping infrastructure on-premises vs. moving to cloud. It considers not just hardware costs, but power, cooling, facilities, staffing, licensing, maintenance, and opportunity cost. A well-built TCO analysis is often the single document that unlocks cloud transformation approval from finance committees and boards.

🔢 The 3-Layer TCO Framework
L1
Direct Infrastructure Costs
Hardware (servers, storage, networking), software licensing, data center lease/build costs, power and cooling, network connectivity. These are the visible costs most organizations track. Map each on-premises cost to its cloud equivalent.
L2
Operational & Hidden Costs
Staff time for maintenance and patching, unplanned downtime costs (revenue loss per hour × MTTR), security incident response costs, compliance audit costs, hardware refresh cycles every 3–5 years. These are often 2–3x larger than direct costs and are the most powerful part of the TCO argument.
L3
Business Value & Opportunity Cost
Speed-to-market improvement (new features deployed in days vs. months), ability to scale for peak demand without over-provisioning, access to AI/ML and advanced services, competitive agility. This layer justifies premium pricing for your advisory work.

📊 Business Case Document Structure
Consulting Deliverable
Section 1 — Executive Summary: Recommendation, 3-year cost comparison (on-prem vs cloud), headline saving figure, ROI percentage and payback period.

Section 2 — Current State Cost Inventory: Itemized on-premises costs across all 3 TCO layers. Include data sources (actual invoices or estimates with assumptions documented).

Section 3 — Proposed Cloud Architecture Cost Model: AWS Pricing Calculator output + reserved instance / savings plan adjustments. Show year 1, 2, 3 separately (year 1 is highest due to migration costs).

Section 4 — Cost Comparison & ROI Analysis: Side-by-side table, NPV analysis, payback period, sensitivity analysis (what if cloud costs are 20% higher than estimated?).

Section 5 — Non-Financial Benefits: Quantified where possible — agility, compliance risk reduction, staff reallocation value.

Section 6 — Risks & Mitigations: Migration risks, cost overrun risks, vendor lock-in considerations, regulatory considerations for data residency in Saudi Arabia.
Module 6 · Weeks 20–23
Cloud Center of Excellence (CCoE) Leadership
🏗️ Organizational Design ⏱ 4 Weeks 🎯 Enterprise Advisory
🏢 What is a CCoE? (From Zero)

A Cloud Center of Excellence is the organizational team responsible for enabling, governing, and scaling cloud adoption across an enterprise. It is part team, part function, part community of practice. Organizations with a mature CCoE adopt cloud 4–5x faster than those without one.

As a consultant, you can be hired to design, stand up, and lead a CCoE as an interim or advisory capacity — this is some of the highest-value and longest-duration consulting work available.

👥
CCoE Team Composition
Cloud Architects (technical leadership), FinOps Practitioners (cost management), Security Engineers (compliance and risk), DevOps Engineers (tooling and automation), Product Owner (prioritization). Typically 5–12 people depending on organization size.
🎯
CCoE Operating Model
Two models: Centralized (CCoE does everything) vs. Federated (CCoE enables product teams who do their own cloud work). Best practice for universities and enterprises: Start centralized, evolve to federated over 12–18 months.
📋
CCoE Charter
Every CCoE needs a charter: mission statement, scope of authority, what they control vs. advise, how they measure success (KPIs), how they engage with other teams, and their budget and headcount.
📈
Measuring CCoE Success
KPIs: Cloud adoption rate (% workloads on cloud), cost optimization savings, time to provision new environments, compliance score (% resources meeting policy), incident reduction rate, developer satisfaction score.
🗺️ How to Stand Up a CCoE — Your Consulting Engagement Plan
1
Month 1 — Assessment & Charter Design
Assess current cloud capability maturity. Interview stakeholders. Design the CCoE charter. Secure executive sponsorship. Define the mandate, budget, and initial team.
2
Month 2–3 — Foundation Building
Establish Landing Zone (AWS Control Tower). Define tagging and cost allocation standards. Create the cloud governance framework. Build the service catalog of approved architectures.
3
Month 4–6 — Enablement & First Wins
Train internal teams. Migrate 2–3 pilot workloads. Demonstrate cost savings and operational improvements. Build internal credibility for the CCoE model.
4
Month 7–12 — Scale & Operationalize
Scale to all business units. Establish regular reporting cadences. Transition to self-service model. Evolve from centralized to federated. Your role shifts from builder to advisor.

⚖️ Risk Advisory Framework for Cloud Consulting
1
Risk Identification
Use the AWS Shared Responsibility Model as your starting map. Categorize risks: Security risks (unauthorized access, data breach), Operational risks (vendor lock-in, outages), Financial risks (cost overrun, license issues), Compliance risks (regulatory violations, data residency).
2
Risk Assessment (Likelihood × Impact)
Build a risk register for each client engagement. Rate each risk: Likelihood (1–5) × Impact (1–5) = Risk Score (1–25). Focus remediation on scores above 12. Present as a heat map to executive audiences.
3
Risk Treatment Selection
For each high risk, recommend: Accept (document and monitor), Mitigate (implement controls), Transfer (insurance, SLAs), or Avoid (change the architecture). Document your rationale — this protects you as a consultant and builds trust with the client.
4
Compliance Gap Analysis
Map the client's current controls against the required framework (e.g., NCA CCC). Identify gaps. Prioritize by risk level and compliance deadline. Build a remediation roadmap with owners and timelines.
Module 8 · Weeks 28–32
AWS Partner Network & Building Your Practice
🤝 Business Development ⏱ 5 Weeks 🎯 Practice Scaling
🌐 AWS Partner Network (APN) — Your Business Accelerator

The AWS Partner Network is the global ecosystem of companies that use AWS to build solutions and services for customers. As an APN partner, you gain access to AWS leads, co-selling opportunities, technical resources, marketing support, and enhanced credibility with enterprise clients. For a Saudi consulting practice, APN status is a significant competitive advantage.

🏅
APN Consulting Partner Tiers
Registered: Entry level, minimal requirements. Select: 2 certified individuals, revenue threshold. Advanced: More certifications, customer references, revenue. Premier: Elite tier, extensive requirements. Start at Registered, target Advanced within 12 months.
🤝
Co-Selling with AWS
APN partners can register client opportunities with AWS. AWS sales teams co-sell with partners, meaning AWS may bring you into deals with their enterprise clients. This is a pipeline multiplier — AWS has relationships with every major Saudi government entity and corporation.
🎓
AWS Competencies
AWS Competencies are earned credentials that validate your practice's depth in specific areas (Government, Education, Security, Migration, etc.). A Government Competency signals expertise to Saudi ministry procurement teams — extremely valuable in your target market.
📣
Partner-Led Marketing
APN partners can co-brand with AWS, appear in AWS partner finder directories, receive marketing development funds (MDF), and participate in AWS events (re:Invent, local summits). This builds visibility in the market without proportional marketing spend.
